The problem

Your eyes are brilliant liars.

Human vision constantly "corrects" what you see. That's why the paint swatch never matches the wall, and the photo never matches the fabric.

💡

Light changes everything

The same object looks warm at sunset, cold under LEDs, greenish under fluorescents. Which one is the "real" color?

🎨

Repainting a wall, reordering fabric, reprinting a batch — every wrong color guess turns into wasted time and material.

The science

Calibrated like the pros do it.

Ambient light fools every camera. Accurate mode fixes it with the same method used in photography and print.

01

18% neutral gray

Frame any standard gray reference card. Because it reflects all wavelengths equally, it reveals the true "white" of your scene's light.

02

Bradford adaptation

A chromatic adaptation in LMS cone space — the same transform used by ICC color management — corrects every reading toward D65 or D50.

03

Honest numbers

Calibrated captures store trustworthy Lab values. And where a phone sensor can't go (metallic, iridescent finishes), the app says so.
